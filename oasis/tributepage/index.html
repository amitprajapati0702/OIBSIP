Early Life

Born as Narendranath Datta on January 12, 1863, in Calcutta, into a progressive Bengali family. His father was an attorney and his mother was deeply religious, shaping his early worldview with both rational thinking and spiritual inclination.

Meeting Ramakrishna

The pivotal moment came when he met Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa in 1881. This encounter transformed his life completely, as he found his spiritual master who would guide him towards his divine mission of serving humanity.

World Parliament

His historic speech at the World's Parliament of Religions in Chicago in 1893 introduced Hinduism and Indian philosophy to the Western world, beginning with the famous words "Sisters and Brothers of America."

Philosophy & Teachings

His teachings emphasized the unity of all religions and the divinity inherent in every human being

Vedanta Philosophy

Vivekananda simplified and modernized Vedanta philosophy, making it accessible to both Eastern and Western minds. He emphasized that the goal of human life is to realize the divinity within oneself.

Legacy & Impact

His influence continues to inspire millions around the world, transcending religious and cultural boundaries

Ramakrishna Mission

Founded the Ramakrishna Mission in 1897, dedicated to serving humanity through education, healthcare, and spiritual guidance.

Educational Reform

Emphasized education that develops both character and intellect, influencing modern educational philosophy in India.

Global Spirituality

Bridged Eastern and Western spiritual traditions, promoting universal acceptance and religious harmony.
